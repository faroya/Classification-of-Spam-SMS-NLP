Scope of my Ph.D Thesis

My Ph.D. thesis is in the field of Signal Processing for the Internet of Things (IoT), under the supervision of Matthieu Puigt and Gilles Roussel, in the University of Littoral Côte d'Opale, Calais, Northern France. My thesis work consists of effectively monitoring air quality using mobile crowdsensing (i.e., a specific way to collect time-stamped and geolocalized data using a crowd of smartphones). In the considered application, the mobile sensors are manufactured and worn by volunteer citizens, and are connected to a central server thanks to the smartphone communication facilities. One major difficulty of this application lies in the fact that these sensors can not be calibrated in a lab before and during deployment. As a consequence, they must be calibrated “in the wild”. Using the idea of sensor rendezvous–which is a standard assumption in mobile sensor calibration and which assumes that sensors in the same spatio-temporal vicinity should observe the same phenomenon–the group where I prepare my Ph.D. thesis revisited the blind calibration problem as an informed (semnon-negative matrix factorization with missing data. One matrix factor then contains the structure of the sensor calibration function (and the sensed phenomenon) while the other contains the calibration parameters. Their proposed techniques take into account the specific knowledge and structure of the problem, i.e., (i) a specific parameterization separating known and free parts of the matrices to estimate, (ii) the sparse approximation of the sensed phenomenon according to a known dictionary of air quality patterns, and (iii) the approximate knowledge of the average calibration parameters. However, these methods are limited to small-sized problems, and the goal of my thesis is to extend them to large-scale problems, involving hundreds of sensors collecting data during years.
In particular, we investigate such approaches for air quality monitoring:

  • in the framework of the OSCAR project, funded by the "Chercheurs Citoyens" program of the Région Hauts-de-France and part of the REPERE program of the French Ecology Ministry, in which we were involved together with ATMO, BES, and Romain ROUVOY's group at Spirals Inria team.
  • in the framework of a CNRS MASTODONS project in which we were involved with Spirals Inria team and the PC2A laboratory.
